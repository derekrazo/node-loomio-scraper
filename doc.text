<p>Now that this group is public by default and the <a href="https://github.com/open-app/core" target="_blank">repo</a> is outside of Enspiral, I&rsquo;d like to get a sense of the Ecosystem comms strategy.</p>

<p>For example, I&rsquo;m aware that other enspiral-like groups are working on, or already have apps similar to what we have proposed. E.g. Camplight (Bulgaria), CoCoon Pro (Italy), Sensorica (Canada-USA), Swedish group (?). </p>

<p>It seems to be beneficial to the ecosystem as a whole if these apps aligned towards the open-app&rsquo;s standards?</p>

<p>Is it our strategy to promote open-app amongst these groups?<br>
If so, what is our value proposition?</p>

<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>It seems to be beneficial to the ecosystem as a whole if these apps aligned towards the open-app’s standards?</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p>Agreed! I have been talking to many groups in the early phases of community-oriented software projects, and the response </p>

<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Is it our strategy to promote open-app amongst these groups? If so, what is our value proposition?</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p>I think making other groups aware of the project, and integrating where possible- and where collaboration seems fluid is a good strategy.</p>

<p>As for value proposition- I think different people will see different aspects of the project as being valuable. </p>

<p>I very much like, &ldquo;a suite of of integrated and open sourced apps which support transparent, democratic and decentralized organizing&rdquo; &ndash; I think friends and activists resonate with this very much.</p>

<p>I&rsquo;m also fond of framing the OAE project in terms of infrastructure. &ldquo;In the same way we need roads, power grids, etc. Society increasingly needs soft infrastructure to support democratic, open and transparent collaboration between individuals, communities, enterprises, municipalities, and governments.&rdquo; &ndash; A (admittedly high level) prop like this might work well for certain grants, programs, etc.</p>

<p>For businesses / startups in the social enterprise, co-op enterprise space I think the simple idea of, &ldquo;We are going to make a bunch of small apps that will make your product easier to develop, cheaper to maintenance, and more coherent with other apps in the space&rdquo; is very compelling (ie. I have had success with it). &ndash; This is especially true if we can carve out collaborations between groups that want similar services and pool resources to feed developers :)</p>

<p>Is this clear / what do you think about these propositions? They are a work in progress :)</p>

<p>Hello! :)) Here&rsquo;re my $0.02:<br>
<a class="tweet-url username" href="#simontegg" rel="nofollow">@simontegg</a> the value we&rsquo;re seeking in Camplight is something along the lines of open source software apps that can help us organize better, make decisions faster, foster transparent culture, mitigate risk clever, thrive together &amp; decentralized and be happy using something that works out of the box :) But Camplight intrinsically is motivated to work towards achieving these goals because well&hellip; we&rsquo;re a collective and realise the values without any further details. I guess other collectives will be more interested in the <strong>features</strong> of such app ecosystem. Currently we&rsquo;ll be trying to make a small <a href="http://176.58.101.229:7777/" target="_blank">&ldquo;launching soon page&rdquo;</a> for our internal bookkeeping software called Moneyflow. We experiment with different kind of copywrite but we think to highlight the <strong>features</strong> more. </p>

<p>I think the current value propositions from <a class="tweet-url username" href="#derekrazo" rel="nofollow">@derekrazo</a> <a class="tweet-url username" href="#alanna" rel="nofollow">@alanna</a>  <a class="tweet-url username" href="#josefdaviescoates" rel="nofollow">@josefdaviescoates</a> are right on spot and it all depends on the context because there&rsquo;re different target segments that the App ecosystem is aiming at. That&rsquo;s why I think that if the value propositions are trying to educate our market to become more open, transparent, flat etc. we should go with more generic stuff. Otherwise the specifics are more meaningful to other organizations such as Enspiral/Camplight (we already know what we seek in terms of value propositions, the deal is that we need the right features in order to integrate)</p>
